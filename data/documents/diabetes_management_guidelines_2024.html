American Diabetes Association Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes—2024

Authors: American Diabetes Association Professional Practice Committee

Publication: Diabetes Care, Volume 47, Supplement 1, January 2024

DOI: 10.2337/dc24-S001

Executive Summary

The American Diabetes Association (ADA) Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes—2024 represents the most current evidence-based recommendations for the diagnosis and management of diabetes mellitus. These guidelines incorporate new evidence from major clinical trials and real-world studies published through 2023.

Key Updates for 2024

  • Expanded recommendations for continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) in type 2 diabetes
  • Updated cardiovascular risk assessment and management strategies
  • New guidance on diabetes technology integration
  • Enhanced focus on health equity and social determinants of health

2. Diagnosis and Classification of Diabetes

2.1 Diagnostic Criteria

Test Normal Prediabetes Diabetes
Fasting Plasma Glucose <100 mg/dL 100-125 mg/dL ≥126 mg/dL
2-hour OGTT <140 mg/dL 140-199 mg/dL ≥200 mg/dL
HbA1c <5.7% 5.7-6.4% ≥6.5%
Random Plasma Glucose N/A N/A ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms

Recommendation 2.1

Grade A: Testing for type 2 diabetes and prediabetes in asymptomatic adults should be considered in adults of any age who are overweight or obese (BMI ≥25 kg/m² or ≥23 kg/m² in Asian Americans) and who have one or more additional risk factors for diabetes.

6. Glycemic Targets

6.1 HbA1c Goals

Recommendation 6.1

Grade A: A reasonable HbA1c goal for many nonpregnant adults with diabetes is <7% (53 mmol/mol).

Recommendation 6.2

Grade C: More stringent HbA1c goals (such as <6.5% [48 mmol/mol]) may be appropriate for selected individuals if this can be achieved without significant hypoglycemia or other adverse effects of treatment.

Recommendation 6.3

Grade B: Less stringent HbA1c goals (such as <8% [64 mmol/mol]) may be appropriate for patients with a history of severe hypoglycemia, limited life expectancy, advanced microvascular or macrovascular complications, extensive comorbid conditions, or long-standing diabetes in whom the goal is difficult to achieve despite diabetes self-management education, appropriate glucose monitoring, and effective doses of multiple glucose-lowering agents including insulin.

6.2 Glucose Monitoring

Recommendation 6.4

Grade A: Real-time continuous glucose monitoring (rtCGM) should be offered for diabetes management to adults with diabetes on intensive insulin therapy (multiple daily injections or insulin pump therapy).

Recommendation 6.5

Grade B: Real-time continuous glucose monitoring may be beneficial for diabetes management in adults with type 2 diabetes not on intensive insulin therapy, particularly those on basal insulin therapy.

9. Pharmacologic Approaches to Glycemic Treatment

9.1 Type 2 Diabetes: Initial Therapy

Recommendation 9.1

Grade A: Metformin is the preferred initial pharmacologic agent for type 2 diabetes unless contraindicated.

Recommendation 9.2

Grade A: Early combination therapy should be considered in patients with newly diagnosed type 2 diabetes with HbA1c ≥9% (75 mmol/mol) to more rapidly achieve glycemic goals.

9.2 Cardiovascular Disease Considerations

Recommendation 9.3

Grade A: In patients with type 2 diabetes and established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease, a sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2) inhibitor or glucagon-like peptide 1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist with demonstrated cardiovascular benefit is recommended as part of the glucose-lowering regimen independent of HbA1c and in consideration of patient-specific factors.

Recommendation 9.4

Grade A: In patients with type 2 diabetes and heart failure with reduced ejection fraction, an SGLT2 inhibitor with demonstrated benefit in heart failure outcomes is recommended as part of the glucose-lowering regimen independent of HbA1c and in consideration of patient-specific factors.

9.3 Kidney Disease Considerations

Recommendation 9.5

Grade A: In patients with type 2 diabetes and diabetic kidney disease, an SGLT2 inhibitor or nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist with evidence of reducing kidney disease progression is recommended as part of the glucose-lowering regimen independent of HbA1c and in consideration of patient-specific factors.

9.4 Weight Management Considerations

Recommendation 9.6

Grade A: When weight loss is a major goal, a GLP-1 receptor agonist or dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist with high efficacy for weight loss is recommended as part of the glucose-lowering regimen.

10. Cardiovascular Disease and Risk Management

10.1 Blood Pressure

Recommendation 10.1

Grade A: For most adults with diabetes and hypertension, a blood pressure target of <130/80 mmHg is appropriate if it can be safely achieved.

Recommendation 10.2

Grade A: For individuals with diabetes and hypertension at higher cardiovascular risk (existing atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease or 10-year atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease risk ≥15%), a blood pressure target of <130/80 mmHg is recommended if it can be safely achieved.

10.2 Lipid Management

Recommendation 10.3

Grade A: Moderate-intensity statin therapy should be initiated or continued for adults with diabetes who are 40-75 years of age.

Recommendation 10.4

Grade A: High-intensity statin therapy is reasonable for adults with diabetes who have multiple atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease risk factors and are 40-75 years of age.

10.3 Antiplatelet Therapy

Recommendation 10.5

Grade A: Use aspirin therapy (75-100 mg/day) as a secondary prevention strategy in those with diabetes and a history of atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease.

7. Diabetes Technology

7.1 Continuous Glucose Monitoring

Recommendation 7.1

Grade A: Adults with diabetes on intensive insulin therapy should be offered continuous glucose monitoring to lower HbA1c levels and/or reduce hypoglycemia.

7.2 Insulin Delivery Systems

Recommendation 7.2

Grade B: Automated insulin delivery systems should be offered to youth and adults with type 1 diabetes who are capable of using the device safely (either by themselves or with a caregiver).

7.3 Digital Health Tools

Recommendation 7.3

Grade B: Digital health tools, including mobile applications and web-based platforms, may be considered as adjuncts to standard diabetes care to support self-management behaviors.

14. Children and Adolescents

Recommendation 14.1

Grade A: HbA1c should be measured quarterly in all children and adolescents with diabetes.

Recommendation 14.2

Grade A: An HbA1c goal of <7% (53 mmol/mol) is recommended for children and adolescents with type 1 diabetes.

Quality Measures

Process Measures

  • HbA1c testing at least twice yearly
  • Annual eye examination
  • Annual foot examination
  • Annual urine microalbumin testing
  • Blood pressure measurement at every visit
  • Lipid profile annually

Outcome Measures

  • HbA1c <7% for most adults
  • Blood pressure <130/80 mmHg
  • LDL cholesterol <100 mg/dL (or <70 mg/dL for high-risk patients)
  • Absence of severe hypoglycemia
  • Absence of diabetic ketoacidosis
